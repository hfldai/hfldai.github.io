I am a DPhil (PhD) student in Interdisciplinary Bioscience at University of Oxford, under the supervision of Gerton Lunter. My research focuses on computational genomics and bioinformatics, which uses statistical methods such as machine learning to understand non-coding functional DNA and gene regulatory networks from next-generation sequencing data. Specifically, I am developing statistical models to improve the interpretation of bulk and single-cell chromatin accessibility data and exploring the causal relationship between cis-regulatory elements in the genome. I also contributed heavily in several collaboration projects within Centre for Computational Biology, MRC Weatherall Institute of Molecular Medicine and the Doctoral Training Centre, such as ComoModels to explore transmission dynamics modelling of COVID-19.

Outside of research, I enjoy playing some random pieces and covers on the piano.
